short-video-bot

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 3, 2026

Overview

short-video-bot is an internal creator tool used to identify public short-form video trends and prepare ranked review reports for editorial planning. The tool is not a public social network, does not offer public account creation, and does not provide TikTok login to public users.

Information processed

When connected to approved platform APIs, short-video-bot may process public video metadata such as video ID, creator username, description, hashtags, duration, public engagement counts, publication date, and public platform URLs.

The tool does not request private TikTok account data, private messages, payment information, precise location data, or sensitive personal information.

How information is used

Public metadata is used only to create internal trend reports, rank candidate clips by relevance and public engagement, and support human editorial review. Recommended links are reviewed by a human before any production decision is made.

Data sharing

short-video-bot does not sell personal data and does not share public video metadata with third-party advertising networks. Data may be stored locally for internal review, debugging, and audit purposes.

Data retention

Internal reports may be retained for as long as needed for editorial planning, compliance review, and operational records. Local data can be deleted by the operator at any time.

Contact

For questions about this policy, contact the operator of the short-video-bot project.
